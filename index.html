Our Mission

Saskatchewan Physicians for Justice and Peace is a physician-led initiative committed to advocacy against violence and supporting just solutions to oppression, genocide, war crimes, and violations of human rights and International Law. It was initiated in response to the ongoing genocide in Gaza, and Israel's longstanding oppression of Palestinians in the form of illegal occupation and annexation of their land. (Since October 2023 over 100,000 Palestinian noncombatants and children have been killed by Israeli military forces and many more impacted by hunger, injury and dispossession. 1700 of our medical colleagues have been killed and many more targeted, imprisoned and tortured.) The organization extends its concerns to other arenas such as the Sudan, where similar genocide, starvation, and forced displacement are ongoing.

As physicians, we recognize the profound health consequences of violence and inequality, and feel we have a unique responsibility to combat these. We believe that peace and justice are fundamental determinants of health and work to advance these. We further recognize our responsibility to medical colleagues — doctors, nurses, ambulance attendants — currently imprisoned and being tortured or have been killed, by the Israeli Defence Forces, while providing medical care to their patients or having their homes targeted. We welcome other healthcare workers to our organization to speak out with us, and engage in evidence-based education and advocacy. We meet virtually on a monthly basis to dialog, educate, advocate and collaborate, and maintain a regularly updated list of educational material on the issues.
